## Technical Implementation

In HideText, we prioritize simplicity and transparency. The core mechanism relies on a classic cryptographic primitive: the bitwise XOR operation, paired with URL-safe encoding for data transport.

### 1. XOR: The Symmetric logic

The foundation is the **Exclusive OR (XOR)** operation. In binary terms, XOR yields `1` only if the inputs are different. Its most critical property for us is being a **self-inverse** or **involution**:

$$ (A \oplus B) \oplus B = A $$

- **Encryption**: We take the UTF-8 byte stream of your plaintext and apply the XOR operator against a random key. 
- **Cycle-Key Logic**: Since your text is often longer than the 8-character key, we use a modulo operation `(i % key.length)` to cycle the key across the entire payload.

This transforms human-readable strings into a raw byte sequence that carries no semantic meaning without the specific key.

### 2. URL-safe Base64 Encoding

Standard Base64 encoding is not inherently safe for URLs because it uses `+`, `/`, and `=`.
- `+` is often interpreted as a space.
- `/` is a path delimiter.

To ensure the ciphertext can be shared safely as a URL parameter (`?c=...`), we apply **URL-safe Base64** (RFC 4648), which replaces `+` with `-` and `/` with `_`. We also strip or handle the padding to keep the URL as clean as possible.

### 3. Client-Side Execution

A crucial technical detail: both `encode.ts` and `deXorStr` functions are executed entirely in the **user's memory (Browser context)**.
